Step 1: Platform Setup
The Guru Promise: "Get your store live in 2-4 hours, even if you've never built a website before!"

The Actual Framework:

Your store lives on Shopify. Not WooCommerce (too technical), not BigCommerce (too enterprise), not Wix (too amateur). Shopify. Every serious dropshipping course points here because the ecosystem is built for exactly this business model.

The Setup Checklist:

Shopify Account:

  • Start with Basic plan ($29/month)
  • Use the 3-day free trial to build, don't pay until you're ready to test ads
  • Skip the annual plan until you've validated

Theme Selection:

Pick a free theme optimized for speed and mobile. The holy trinity:

  • Dawn (Shopify's flagship free theme, clean, fast)
  • Craft (better for lifestyle products)
  • Refresh (solid for health/beauty niches)

Paid themes ($180-350) are unnecessary at this stage. Anyone who tells you otherwise is selling themes.

Domain:

  • Use Shopify's domain registration ($14/year) or connect from Namecheap/GoDaddy
  • Avoid hyphens, numbers, and anything that requires spelling out loud
  • .com only. Not .store, not .shop, not .io. Just .com.

Psychology tip: Shorter domains convert better. BrightHome.com beats BrightHomeEssentialsStore.com.

Essential Pages:

  • Homepage — Hero image, value proposition, featured products
  • Product pages — Built per product (covered in Step 4)
  • About Us — Brand story (can be fictional for DTC brands)
  • Contact — Email form + email address visible
  • Shipping Policy — Realistic timelines (this matters later)
  • Refund Policy — Shopify has generators for this
  • Privacy Policy — Legal requirement, Shopify generates this too

Payment Processing:

  • Shopify Payments (if available in your country) — lowest fees
  • Stripe as backup
  • PayPal (customers expect it, despite the fees)
Setup is the easy part. It's also where most people stop feeling optimistic.
Step 2: Product Research
The Guru Promise: "Find winning products using our proprietary research methodology!"

The Actual Framework:

There is no proprietary methodology. There's a checklist everyone uses.

Research Tools:

Free:

  • AliExpress "Top Selling" sections
  • TikTok search (look for viral product videos)
  • Amazon Movers & Shakers
  • Google Trends (validate trajectory)
  • Facebook Ad Library (spy on competitors)

Paid ($29-99/month):

  • Ecomhunt
  • Sell The Trend
  • Dropship Spy
  • Niche Scraper

The paid tools aggregate what you could find manually. They save time. They don't guarantee winners.

Validation Protocol:

Before committing to a product:

  1. Google Trends — Is interest rising, stable, or dying?
  2. Facebook Ad Library — How many competitors are running ads? (Some competition = validated market. Too much = bloodbath.)
  3. AliExpress Orders — Has the supplier moved 1,000+ units? (Proof of fulfillment capability)
  4. TikTok/Instagram Search — Is there organic content? (Free marketing potential)
Finding a "winner" is the easy part. What happens next separates the 10% from the 90%.
Step 3: Supplier Connection
The Guru Promise: "Connect with reliable suppliers with one click!"

The Actual Framework:

Your supplier is your invisible business partner. You'll never meet them. You'll rarely speak with them. They control your customer experience entirely.

The Platform Hierarchy:

AliExpress (Starting Point):

  • Largest selection
  • Longest shipping times
  • Integration via DSers app (free tier available)
  • Good for: Testing product viability

CJ Dropshipping (Growth Stage):

  • Faster shipping via regional warehouses
  • Requires account approval
  • Can source products you find elsewhere
  • Good for: Products you've validated

Spocket (Premium Play):

  • US/EU suppliers = faster delivery
  • Higher product costs, lower margins
  • Good for: Markets that won't tolerate 3-week shipping

Supplier Vetting Protocol:

Before partnering with any supplier:

Check Minimum Standard
Rating 4.7+ stars
Orders 1,000+ total
Response Time Under 24 hours
Product Photos Professional, not stock
Communication Clear English (enough to resolve issues)
Non-Negotiable: Order a sample first. Always. Every time. No exceptions.

Integration Setup:

  1. Install DSers (Shopify App Store, free tier)
  2. Connect AliExpress account
  3. Import products to store
  4. Set pricing rules (auto-markup)
  5. Enable auto-ordering (optional—some prefer manual control)

Communication Templates:

Bulk Discount Request:

"Hello, I'm testing [product] for my store and expect 50-100 orders/month initially, scaling to 500+. What pricing can you offer for bulk orders? I'm evaluating multiple suppliers."

Shipping Timeline Confirmation:

"Before I proceed, please confirm: What is the realistic shipping time to USA/UK/Canada using ePacket? I need accurate timelines for my customers."

Custom Packaging Inquiry:

"Do you offer custom packaging or branded inserts? What are minimum order quantities and additional costs?"
Your supplier becomes your silent business partner. Choose wisely—you won't always have a choice later.
Step 4: Store Optimization
The Guru Promise: "Copy-paste your way to a high-converting store!"

The Actual Framework:

Your store needs to overcome one fundamental problem: you're asking strangers to give their credit card number to a website they've never heard of, for a product they've never touched, from a company with no reputation.

Trust signals do the heavy lifting.

The Trust Signal Stack:

Reviews (Critical):

  • App: Judge.me (free tier) or Loox (photo reviews)
  • Import reviews from AliExpress (gray area, everyone does it)
  • Request reviews post-purchase via email automation

Trust Badges:

  • Secure checkout badges
  • Money-back guarantee badge
  • "Safe & Secure" icons near checkout button
  • Payment method logos (Visa, Mastercard, PayPal)

Live Chat:

  • App: Tidio (free tier) or Shopify Inbox
  • Doesn't need to be 24/7—set expectations
  • Quick responses during testing phase = higher conversion

Clear Policies:

  • Shipping: Honest timelines (do NOT say "3-5 days" if it's 15-25)
  • Returns: 30-day money-back is standard
  • FAQ: Pre-answer objections

Essential Apps:

Purpose App Cost
Reviews Judge.me Free tier
Email Capture Klaviyo Free under 250 contacts
Upsells ReConvert $4.99/mo
Urgency Ultimate Sales Boost Free tier
Live Chat Tidio Free tier

Product Page Formula:

Every high-converting product page follows this structure:

  1. Headline: Benefit-focused, not feature-focused
    ❌ "Posture Corrector with Adjustable Straps"
    ✅ "Finally Fix Your Back Pain in 30 Days"
  2. Hero Image: Product in use, lifestyle context
  3. Pain → Agitate → Solve:
    • Identify the problem
    • Twist the knife (what happens if they don't solve it)
    • Present your product as the solution
  4. Benefits Section: 3-5 bullet points, icon-enhanced
  5. Social Proof Block: Reviews, testimonials, user photos
  6. FAQ Section: Handle objections before they become abandoned carts
  7. CTA Button: Clear, contrasting color, above the fold
A beautiful store with perfect trust signals. Now you just need one thing: people to see it.
Step 5: Ad Launch
The Guru Promise: "Start with just $10-20/day and scale from there!"

The Actual Framework:

This is where money meets algorithm. Everything before this was setup. This is the game.

Platform Selection:

Facebook/Instagram (Still King):

  • Largest audience, best targeting
  • Higher CPMs than 2019, still viable
  • Requires Business Manager setup
  • Pixel installation critical

TikTok (Rising Contender):

  • Younger demographic
  • Lower CPMs currently
  • Creative-hungry (needs fresh content constantly)
  • Spark Ads (boost organic) = cheat code

Google (Advanced):

  • Search intent = higher conversion
  • Requires keyword research
  • Harder for impulse products
  • Better for problem-solution products

Facebook Ads Fundamentals:

Campaign Structure:

Campaign (CBO - Campaign Budget Optimization) ├── Ad Set 1 (Interest: Yoga enthusiasts) │ ├── Ad 1 (Video) │ ├── Ad 2 (Carousel) │ └── Ad 3 (Static image) ├── Ad Set 2 (Interest: Back pain) │ ├── Ad 1 (Video) │ ├── Ad 2 (Carousel) │ └── Ad 3 (Static image) └── Ad Set 3 (Interest: Office workers) ├── Ad 1 (Video) ├── Ad 2 (Carousel) └── Ad 3 (Static image)

Testing Framework:

Phase Daily Budget Duration Goal
Testing $30-50 3-5 days Find winning creative + audience
Validation $50-100 5-7 days Confirm profitability
Scaling $100+ Ongoing Maximize profitable spend

Kill/Scale Metrics:

Metric Kill Signal Scale Signal
CTR (Click-Through Rate) <1% after $10 spend >2%
CPC (Cost Per Click) >$2.00 <$1.00
Add to Cart Rate <3% >8%
Purchase ROAS <1.5x after 3 days >2.5x

Creative That Converts:

Video outperforms static. The formula:

  1. Hook (0-3 sec): Pattern interrupt, stop the scroll
  2. Problem (3-10 sec): "Do you struggle with..."
  3. Solution (10-20 sec): Product demo
  4. Social Proof (20-25 sec): Reviews, testimonials
  5. CTA (25-30 sec): "Click below to get yours"

Budget Reality:

Phase Realistic Budget
Testing (finding winners) $500-1,500
Validation (confirming) $500-1,000
Initial Scale $1,000-3,000/month
Minimum to Start Properly $2,000-3,000
The "$5/day" narrative is technically possible. It's also why most people quit before finding a winner—they run out of runway during testing.
The algorithm giveth. The algorithm taketh away. Often in the same week.
Step 6: Scale to Six Figures
The Guru Promise: "Once you find a winner, just increase your budget and watch the money roll in!"

The Actual Framework:

Scaling is not "spend more money." Scaling is navigating a maze where the walls move.

The Math of "Six Figures":

Here's what the gurus mean—and what they leave out:

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) Targets:

ROAS Status Action
<1.5x Losing money Kill or optimize
1.5-2x Break-even zone Proceed with caution
2-2.5x Marginally profitable Optimize before scaling
2.5-3x Healthy Scale carefully
3x+ Strong Scale aggressively

Automation Stack:

Once validated, systematize:

Function Tool Purpose
Abandoned Cart Klaviyo Recover 5-15% of lost sales
Post-Purchase Upsell ReConvert Increase AOV 10-20%
Review Requests Judge.me Build social proof
Restock Reminders Klaviyo Repeat purchases
Notice we said revenue, not profit. That distinction will cost some readers everything.
